Here are a few stories about stuff that has happened in clubs. Warning: may be quite heavily skewed.

let's start with the one i'm most salty about: math league

some background on the club: it primarily just runs competitions, basically NJML, AMC, AIME (when we have qualifiers). We also used to run purplecomet, math madness, and actually went to PUMaC.

Back in freshman year, it became quite apparent that our grade was going to be the strongest at math, and it definitely had the most potential. That year, I went to most of the events, and didn't go to PUMaC. I believe PUMaC team that year was AL, AL, BZ, AP, RC, and i don't really remember the rest. Anyway they awarded me some "competition manager" or some random role.

Sophomore year, I continued attending, etc. AL actually missed PUMaC registration because of the date change, which is unfortunate. I found another team, which was nice. Anyway, I was pretty lazy so on my exec board application I just wrote "we will go to pumac :D" or something along those lines. Well, when they made decisions, NZ decided I was too cracked, and also saw my "essay", and didn't give me any eboard positions. Well guess what happened to going to pumac :P.

Instead, the positions went to ML, AP, AA, and DW. It became apparent pretty soon that DW didn't really have power (well, he only cares about AMC, which is lowk very valid, esp with AP and AA running the club; as such he doesn't have any power, and for the remainder, I will not group him with eboard). And ML didn't care about anything. For reference, neither of AP and AA do comp math, and they can't even solve the sample problems that they present at the club presentation.

Anyway, I'm a bit salty, but I really don't care. At that point I stopped caring about this kind of stuff anyway. So junior year rolls by, and the eboard not only misses PUMaC again, but also like didn't really do anything. At this point, I don't care about the club, and neither do any of the math oly kids at our school. But it is quite sad that we didn't go to PUMaC at all. We also didn't do math madness like last year.

So senior year happens. Well, I already predicted that we wouldn't go to PUMaC, so I found a team at our school, luckily. Indeed, our school did not go to PUMaC, at least not the math league. And instead of going to PUMaC, what happens is that the eboard (by this i mean AA and AP) hosts a basketball tournament on the same day of PUMaC (with some morally questionable stuff like charging registration fees and implying leadership positions.) And it appears that the leadership positions will all be nepo'd next year to non-math-oly kids.

<p style="text-align:center;">* * *</p>

Upon writing this, it appears to be not-so-atypical. Or at least, it shouldn't have been surprising (well, it wasn't, but still...). But, it is kinda sad that for the math oly kids in our grade (and in other grades too), we didn't go to PUMaC (except worse walrus), or any other fun team math contests. Or run anything like FTW tournaments or the like. Math league (probably) was like actually doing something before (modulo covid), and now it's reduced to just this, i guess.

And instead, whoever's running this is really just statpadding, and doesn't care about any of this. This is why math oly kids should run this, at least they care, somewhat. Most people don't find solving or groupsolving math problems fun, and that's ok, but they shouldn't be running this kind of club, then.

Anyway, I don't really care about ts anymore, math league is probably cooked for the near future. At least, I'd bet that PUMaC isn't happening.

Fun fact: somehow, a nontrivial subset of math league hallucinates that I attend meetings, and some people even think I'm the president. Even though I haven't been at a math league meeting for over a year.
