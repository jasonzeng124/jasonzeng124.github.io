anyway enough yap. the blog post was originally titled "don't learn from chatgpt" (now colloquially referred to as "chat" for some reason). 

So while I was TAing for physics i noticed that like almost everyone was using some LLMs to learn or something. This is usually divided into like two not-necessarily-intersecting categories: blatant "do my homework" and actually attempting to learn from LLMs. The former is usually just stupid (unless you already know everything / you don't want to do computational problems / whatever), but the latter is quite bad for you as well.

Why? It's really easy to read something that LLMs write, agree with it, and move on thinking that you learned something, whereas in fact you have not at all advanced your understanding. It doesn't help that you can ask the LLMs questions, because it will output something that you think it makes sense, and maybe it does make sense, but it's easy for you to read it without internalizing it.

You end up with a sort of superficial understanding which makes enough sense for you at the moment, and maybe it's enough for you to pass whatever class or whatever, but because you haven't actually thought about it, you don't actually understand it for the most part.

But this isn't unique to LLMs. Having a teacher will likewise have the same problem. Or even reading a textbook. Although a teacher will have somewhat less of the problem if they are good (e.g. teaching things the morally correct way, anticipating misunderstandings, and having a good grasp of the student's understanding), and textbooks also do things like inline problems/exercises to make sure you are understanding. 

The important part is to actually think about what you are supposed to be learning. this includes things like doing problems or answering any "exercises to the reader" (they are usually actually worth thinking about, not just the author being lazy, or at least for good textbooks). I question every few lines of the textbook (and often in the process I find something that I don't understand).

So reading a textbook is not simply "read every word of the textbook" :D.

And I would be especially careful around LLMs. They tend to be very easy to agree with, which here, is a bad thing.
